Cyberbullying :-

Cybercrime is the most trending thing in our country. As it is often said, with great responsibility comes more risks. We are too busy to post about our personal life on social media that we do not understand the effects of it. Cyberbullying is one of such crimes. In this crime, the person harasses and threaten the other person using their images and videos which they gathered from various social media and other platforms. It is considered as a crime in the eyes of law as it harasses and threaten the victims. Sometimes, victims may think to end their life due to these harassments.



A girl used to post about herself on social media. The account was public so a group of people who were not their friends started making a group to bully her. They used to comment bad on her post, make accounts with her picture and comment on other people post. They used to make her edited photos and write bad things. Then they started showing the posts to class. Everyone started being distanced from her, ignore her and she had no friends left. This let her confidence down and her marks started decreasing and she stopped coming school.

How this happens-



•	Scammer starts gathering the information such as appearance, friend circle and everything.


•	Starts sending them bad messages and posting ai made videos.


•	The other people also join and start commenting.


•	The victim tries to message and ask him to stop but in return just gets blackmailed by them.




•	The public reaction makes it impossible to forget the comments and post and at last it creates a life- long trauma for them.

Targeted audience is-


•	Teenagers 


•	People whose account is public


•	Celebrities


•	Adults 



The consequences are very bad. Victims mostly try to end their life or experience anxiety and depression. They stop going to places and isolates themselves from the world. Cyberbullying is a serious digital crime with lasting emotional and social impacts.
